Phishing Awareness & Protection

  • Home
  • Team
  • Support

Phishing is a social engineering attack where malicious actors impersonate trusted entities to steal personal data such as usernames, passwords, and financial details. It’s the #1 method used in cybercrime globally — including here in Kenya.

🎯 How Phishing Works

  • You receive an email or SMS claiming to be from a bank, employer, or social media site.
  • The message urges you to click a link or download an attachment urgently.
  • You’re taken to a fake site that looks real — but is designed to steal your credentials or infect your device.

🧪 Kenyan Examples

  • Equity Bank scam emails: Attackers used spoofed domains to lure users into resetting their PINs on fake pages.
  • Safaricom fake Bonga Points promos: Phishing SMS redirecting users to credential harvesting sites.
  • NTSA fake invoice phishing: Victims received realistic PDFs with malware payloads.

🚩 Common Signs of Phishing

  • Urgent messages like “Your account will be closed!”
  • Suspicious sender addresses (e.g. support@paypai.com)
  • Grammatical or spelling errors
  • Unexpected attachments or shortened URLs (e.g. bit.ly links)
  • Login forms that don’t use HTTPS

🛡️ How to Stay Safe

  1. Never click on suspicious links or download unknown attachments.
  2. Verify URLs — legit sites always start with https:// and proper domain.
  3. Use a password manager to detect impostor sites.
  4. Enable 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication) on all accounts.
  5. Report phishing to reportphishing@apwg.org.
