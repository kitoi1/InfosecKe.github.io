Understanding Encryption

  • Home
  • Mission
  • Contact

Encryption is the foundation of cybersecurity. It's the process of converting data into a form that cannot be read without a special key. From messaging apps to banking, encryption protects everything.

🔐 Why It Matters

Imagine sending a letter through the post. Without encryption, anyone along the way can read it. With encryption, it’s locked in a box that only the recipient can open.

🔄 Types of Encryption

1. Symmetric Encryption

Uses one secret key for both encryption and decryption. Fast and efficient, used in AES (Advanced Encryption Standard).

2. Asymmetric Encryption

Uses a pair of keys: public (to encrypt) and private (to decrypt). Common in HTTPS, Bitcoin, and PGP.

📱 Real-World Use Cases

  • 🔒 WhatsApp: End-to-end encrypted messages using Signal Protocol.
  • 💳 Online Banking: SSL/TLS protects data between browser and bank server.
  • ₿ Bitcoin: Private/public keys secure your cryptocurrency.
