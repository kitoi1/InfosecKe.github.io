Studying real-world attacks helps cybersecurity professionals understand how threats evolve and what defenses work. Here are some of the most impactful cases from the past decade:

☢️ Stuxnet (2010)

Impact: Targeted Iranian nuclear centrifuges via Siemens SCADA systems. First known cyberweapon with physical destruction.

Wired's in-depth Stuxnet story →

🌐 SolarWinds Supply Chain Attack (2020)

Impact: Compromised updates in SolarWinds Orion platform affected over 18,000 organizations, including U.S. federal agencies.

CISA Official SolarWinds Guidance →

📲 Pegasus Spyware (2016–2021)

Impact: A mobile spyware from NSO Group that could silently infect iOS/Android phones and extract messages, mic, camera data.

Amnesty Pegasus Project →

💸 WannaCry Ransomware (2017)

Impact: Encrypted files in 150+ countries. Used leaked NSA exploit "EternalBlue" against unpatched Windows machines.

UK NCSC Official Summary →

🔓 Equifax Breach (2017)

Impact: Personal and financial data of 147 million people leaked. Cause: Unpatched Apache Struts vulnerability (CVE-2017-5638).

FTC Case Details →

🕵️‍♂️ Kenya: Huduma Namba & eCitizen Attacks

Impact: Data privacy concerns and temporary outages affected national platforms. Reinforced the need for robust national cyber defenses.

Read: Kenya's Digital ID Concerns

Follow: Kenya Office of Data Protection

  • 🚨 Even nation-states can fall victim to poor patching & supply chain attacks
  • 🧠 Cyberwarfare includes silent, persistent espionage—not just ransomware
  • 🔐 Secure coding, segmentation, patching, and threat intel are critical
