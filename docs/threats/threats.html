Cybersecurity threats continue to evolve in scale and sophistication. This page introduces the core types of threats facing individuals, businesses, governments, and infrastructures today.

🔥 Major Categories of Threats

  • Malware – Viruses, Trojans, ransomware, worms
  • Phishing & Social Engineering – Tricking users into revealing sensitive info
  • DDoS Attacks – Overwhelming systems to cause downtime
  • Zero-Day Exploits – Attacks before a patch is available
  • Insider Threats – Employees or partners leaking or abusing access
  • Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs) – Long-term, stealthy state-sponsored attacks

📊 Global Threat Trends (2024–2025)

According to Verizon's DBIR and other security vendors:

  • Ransomware is the #1 threat for small and medium businesses (SMBs)
  • Phishing accounts for 70% of initial access vectors
  • Misconfiguration in cloud environments is on the rise
  • Africa and Southeast Asia are increasingly targeted

🕵️ Real-World Case: WannaCry Ransomware (2017)

The WannaCry outbreak affected 230,000+ computers globally, exploiting a Windows SMBv1 vulnerability (EternalBlue). Hospitals, banks, and transport systems were brought down. It highlighted the need for timely patching and backups.

🔐 How to Stay Protected

  • Keep software and systems updated regularly
  • Use strong, unique passwords and enable 2FA
  • Back up critical data frequently
  • Educate your team on phishing and social engineering
  • Use endpoint protection and firewall systems
