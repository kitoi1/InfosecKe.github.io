Common Attack Vectors

  • Home
  • About
  • Support

An attack vector is the method or pathway a hacker uses to access or exploit a system. Understanding these vectors is essential to protect yourself and your organization from cyber threats.

🚨 1. Phishing Emails

Attackers send fraudulent emails designed to trick recipients into revealing personal data or clicking malicious links.

Read more about phishing →

💾 2. Infected USB Devices

USB drops rely on human curiosity. When a malicious USB is plugged in, it executes hidden scripts or malware.

🌐 3. Drive-By Downloads

Visiting a compromised or malicious website can lead to automatic downloading of malware without your consent.

👨‍💼 4. Insider Threats

Employees or partners with legitimate access misuse their credentials to steal data or sabotage systems.

CISA on Insider Threats →

⚙️ 5. Unpatched Vulnerabilities

Attackers often exploit known software vulnerabilities (CVEs) that haven’t been patched in time. This was the method behind the famous Equifax breach.

🔌 6. Misconfigured Services

Open ports, unsecured APIs, and misconfigured cloud storage (e.g., open S3 buckets) are common entry points.

📱 7. Mobile App Exploits

Fake apps or outdated legitimate apps can contain backdoors, spyware, or allow privilege escalation on mobile devices.

🎥 Watch: Top 10 Cyber Attack Vectors

🛡️ How to Defend Against These Vectors

  • 🔐 Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA)
  • 📬 Train users to detect phishing and suspicious links
  • 🔄 Regularly update and patch all software
  • 🛠️ Audit and secure misconfigured services
  • 💼 Use least privilege access for employees
  • 📊 Conduct regular penetration testing and security assessments
