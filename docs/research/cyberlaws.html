Kenya Cyber Laws & Digital Policy

Explore the regulatory and legal framework governing cybersecurity, data privacy, and digital rights in Kenya.

1. The Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act, 2018

This is the primary law for addressing offenses like hacking, cyberbullying, identity theft, and unauthorized access. It also outlines procedures for investigations and cooperation with international agencies.

Read the Full Act

2. Data Protection Act, 2019

This Act governs the collection, processing, and storage of personal data. It created the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) to enforce compliance and handle data breach incidents.

View the Data Protection Act

3. ICT Policy Guidelines

The Ministry of ICT periodically issues national guidelines on cybersecurity strategies, public-private partnerships, and digital infrastructure protection. These policies align with Vision 2030 goals.

Visit Ministry of ICT

4. International Cyber Law Treaties

Kenya is a member of the East African Communications Organization (EACO), and it participates in international cybercrime conventions such as the African Union Convention on Cybersecurity (Malabo Convention).

Read AU Cyber Convention

5. Other Related Laws

  • Kenya Information and Communications Act (KICA)
  • Evidence Act – Admissibility of digital evidence
  • Banking and Financial Sector Guidelines – CBK cybersecurity framework
