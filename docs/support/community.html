Our Community

Welcome to the InfoSecKe Cybersecurity Community

InfoSecKe is more than an initiative — it’s a living, breathing community of cybersecurity enthusiasts, ethical hackers, students, developers, defenders, bug bounty hunters, and information security professionals. Our mission goes beyond technology; it's about uniting minds around a shared vision of a safer digital future for Kenya and Africa.

Why Community Matters

In cybersecurity, knowledge grows exponentially when shared. That’s why we built InfoSecKe as an open platform where members can learn, collaborate, compete, and contribute together. Whether you're a newcomer trying to understand OWASP Top 10, a SOC analyst defending real infrastructure, or a pentester disclosing vulnerabilities — your voice is essential.

What Makes Our Community Unique

  • Inclusive for All Levels: We welcome everyone — from absolute beginners to seasoned experts — and support growth at every stage.
  • Ethical and Responsible: We uphold the highest standards of responsible disclosure and ethical behavior.
  • Built on Collaboration: Our platforms encourage peer-to-peer mentorship, team-based CTFs, and collaborative research.
  • Locally Relevant: We tailor our content and tools to match the unique cyber landscape of Kenya and East Africa.

Community Features

  1. Forums and Groups: Public and private channels for discussing everything from vulnerability research to job opportunities.
  2. Bug Bounty Directory: A curated list of local and global bounty programs with community-submitted writeups.
  3. Mentorship Program: Connect with a mentor in red teaming, blue teaming, reverse engineering, or DevSecOps.
  4. HackTogether Events: Participate in group-based hacking sprints, web app challenges, and lab walkthroughs.
  5. Local Meetups: Monthly hybrid (online + offline) events featuring workshops, keynotes, and networking.
  6. Women in Cyber: A safe space and initiative to support, train, and promote women in Kenyan cybersecurity.
  7. CTF League: Compete in our monthly Capture the Flag (CTF) events — solo or as a team — with rankings and prizes.
  8. Contribute to Open Source: Help us build tools like VulnHunter Pro, KasauXSS, and our upcoming Auto-Vuln Lab Generator.

Ongoing Projects You Can Join

  • InfoSecKe Labs: Hands-on challenge environments to practice real-world exploitation.
  • Cyber Awareness Outreach: Help us teach digital hygiene in schools and rural communities.
  • Research Publications: Contribute whitepapers, exploit breakdowns, and threat intelligence reports.
  • Tool Development: Collaborate on building or improving our reconnaissance and exploitation tools.

Join Us

Ready to dive in? Becoming part of InfoSecKe is simple:

  1. Sign up and build your profile via our Register page.
  2. Introduce yourself in the #introductions channel.
  3. Pick a challenge, contribute to a project, or apply for mentorship.

Community Code of Conduct

We are committed to maintaining a respectful, harassment-free environment for all members. Harassment of any kind will not be tolerated. Be kind, stay curious, and help others grow.
